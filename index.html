CELL PHONE SUBSCRIPTIONS PER COUNTRY


This map shows how many cell phone subscriptions there are per 100 people in each country of the world, according to data from 2011 compiled by the International Telecommunication Union, the World Telecommunication/ICT Development Report and database, and the World Bank.The countries with the most cell phone subscriptions per 100 people are Antigua and Barbuda with 196 subscriptions per 100 people, Saudi Arabia with 191 subscriptions per 100 people, and Panama with 189 subscriptions per 100 people.

To discover how many cell phone subscriptions other countries have, roll your cursor over the interactive map below.

Note: Mobile cellular telephone subscriptions are subscriptions to a public mobile telephone service using cellular technology, which provide access to the public switched telephone network. Post-paid and prepaid subscriptions are included.
Visualization by Annie P. Waldman, 2014.
